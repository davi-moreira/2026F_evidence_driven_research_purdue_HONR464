## Purpose

On Nov 17 a live audience stands in front of this poster, not a draft reader. The
production sprint converts your triaged draft into a print-ready file where every
number traces to a notebook cell and every claim sits inside your compass
position's boundary. "I'll fix it after submission" is the one move this session exists to
prevent.

## Materials

- Your triaged draft poster (all M30 hits applied).
- The URC poster spec sheet (dimensions, orientation, and format per **URC
  guidance on Brightspace** — confirm the current numbers there, do not guess).
- Your pilot notebook, runnable, open beside the poster.

## Print-spec checks (against the URC spec sheet on Brightspace)

- ☐ Physical dimensions and orientation match URC guidance *(fill from the spec sheet — do not assume last year's numbers).*
- ☐ Body text and figure labels are ≥ the minimum legible size **at final print scale** (view at 100%, not zoomed to fit the screen).
- ☐ Title readable from the far side of a room; headline claim readable from a step back.
- ☐ Figures exported at print resolution (no pixelation when scaled up).
- ☐ File format and naming match the submission instructions exactly.

## Number-to-notebook traceability (the evidence-integrity gate)

Every number on the poster must come from a cell you can point to. This is the
CLAUDE.md Evidence-Integrity rule, enforced on paper.

**Spot-check procedure (pairs, \~10 min):**
1. Pick one number on your poster (your partner may pick a different one for you).
2. Open your notebook and show the exact cell that produced it — value matches.
3. Your partner initials the traceability line on your checklist.
4. Swap. You verify one of your partner's numbers the same way.

If a number does not trace, it does not ship. Recompute it or cut it.

## Uncertainty-visible check

- ☐ The uncertainty line is on the poster and legible without leaning in.
- ☐ Every figure shows its uncertainty or carries its caveat.
- ☐ The headline claim's bound sits in the same eye-span as the claim.

## Submission hygiene

- ☐ Filename per instructions; correct file, final version (not an autosave).
- ☐ Submitted to Brightspace **and** the URC channel as instructed.
- ☐ Submission confirmation captured (screenshot or receipt).
- ☐ AI-use disclosure block present on the dossier record for this artifact (tool, task, verification).

Professor Moreira keeps the consult rotation to \~5 minutes each, confirms the

## The print backup plan

If printing or the portal fails: submit the **PDF to Brightspace** — that
timestamp meets the Nov 6 deadline. Physical printing is then resolved in the
following week, before Nov 17. A print failure never becomes a missed deadline;
the file is what is due today, the paper is what is needed by the Expo.

## Common failure modes

1. **Deferring the fix.** There is no after. The stranger on Nov 17 reads the submitted version.
2. **Fonts sized to the screen, not the print.** Check at 100% zoom / full print scale, or the room won't read it.
3. **A number that doesn't trace.** If you can't show the cell, cut the number — an untraceable figure fails the evidence-integrity gate.
4. **Uncertainty lost in the final layout.** Re-confirm it survived the last design pass.
5. **Submitting the wrong file.** Verify it's the final version, correctly named, before you close the laptop.
