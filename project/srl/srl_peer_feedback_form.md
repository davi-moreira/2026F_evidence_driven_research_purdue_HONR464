# Peer Feedback for the Student Research Lead

*You fill this out for whoever led today. It takes under three minutes, and it is
the fastest way your classmate gets better before their next slot. Be specific
and be kind. Point at moments, not personalities. "Your question about the
control group made me rethink my answer" helps; "good job" does not.*

### 1. One thing that worked

*A specific strength. What did the lead do that you want to steal for your own
slot?*



### 2. One Socratic question that landed

*Quote or paraphrase the single question that actually made the room think or
changed your mind.*



### 3. One moment the AI got interrogated well

*Where did the lead push on an AI answer instead of accepting it? A source
checked, a confident claim questioned, a missing uncertainty caught. If it did
not happen, say "did not see one" — that is useful too.*



### 4. One suggestion for next time

*The single most useful change. Frame it as a next step, not a complaint. "Give
us five more seconds to commit before opening Gemini" is perfect.*
