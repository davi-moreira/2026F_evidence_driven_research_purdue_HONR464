# Research Brief Template — M40–M41 (Dec 2 & Dec 4)

*The one-page brief a decision-maker reads in 90 seconds. Governs
[milestone_21 — Research Brief](../../_research_project/2026Fall/milestone_21_research_brief.md).
Drafted in class M40, workshopped M41 (table read); due Fri Dec 4.*

## Purpose

Your dossier carries everything; your brief carries what a busy reader with
decision power needs, on one page, with the uncertainty ON the page — not in an
appendix they will never open. The brief is the dossier's honest ambassador:
every sentence traces to a claim-ledger row, and it compresses the finding
without ever un-verifying it.

## The genre rules

1. **One page. One reader. One figure.** Name the reader; write to them.
2. **Compresses, never un-verifies.** You may shorten; you may not restore a
   caveat-free claim the ledger already disciplined. If polish upgrades the
   claim, upgrade the ledger first — with evidence.
3. **Uncertainty on the page.** The one bound the reader must know sits in the
   finding, not in fine print.
4. **Every sentence traces to a ledger row.** If a sentence has no row, either
   cut it or add the row (with its evidence).

## The template (fill every block)

```
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RESEARCH BRIEF                         [Your name] · [Date]

READER:  ____________________________________________________
  (who decides something with this — name the role and what they need)

QUESTION:  __________________________________________________
  (one sentence: units, outcome, comparison, scope visible)

FINDING (with uncertainty on the page):
  ___________________________________________________________
  ___________________________________________________________
  (the claim inside its compass-position boundary + its bound, in the same breath)

IMPLICATION:  _______________________________________________
  (what the reader can do / conclude — sized to the evidence, not beyond)

THE ONE FIGURE:
  ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  [ the single figure that carries the finding —        │
  │    honest axes, uncertainty shown or captioned ]       │
  └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ledger-traceability footer:
  Finding → ledger row #___    Uncertainty → row #___
  Implication → row #___       Figure number → notebook cell ___
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## The finding block (the hardest compression)

The finding must say the claim AND its bound in one readable move — this is the
compression that keeps the caveat. Practice (nb18): take a three-sentence
caveated finding and compress it to one sentence WITHOUT losing the caveat.

- ✓ *"In our sample of N, X was associated with Y (about ±Z); this is an
  association, not a cause."*
- ⚠️ *"X drives Y."* — un-verified: dropped the frame and upgraded to cause.

## The table-read margin-note procedure (M41)

All five briefs are read by all five people. Each brief passes left; **3 minutes
per read**; on each you leave a two-question margin note:

```
MARGIN NOTE          Brief author: __________  Reader: __________
Sharpest sentence:  _______________________________________
Weakest claim:      _______________________________________
```

After the full rotation, you revise from the margin notes — then prep your three
weakest ledger rows for the Evidence Defense.

- **Reader (in the table read):** name one sharpest sentence and one weakest claim per brief — specific, on paper.

## Quality checklist (before submitting M21)

- ☐ One page; a named reader; one figure.
- ☐ Finding states its claim AND its uncertainty in the same move.
- ☐ No sentence upgrades a claim past its ledger row.
- ☐ The traceability footer maps every block to a row and the figure to a cell.
- ☐ Revised from at least one margin note.

## Common failure modes

1. **Un-verifying under polish.** Smooth prose that quietly drops the caveat. If the wording upgrades the claim, the ledger must be upgraded first.
2. **Uncertainty in the appendix.** The reader has 90 seconds; the bound goes on the page.
3. **No named reader.** A brief to "whom it may concern" serves no one. Name the decision.
4. **Two figures, or none.** One figure, chosen to carry the finding.
5. **A sentence with no row.** If it can't trace to the ledger, cut it or earn it.
