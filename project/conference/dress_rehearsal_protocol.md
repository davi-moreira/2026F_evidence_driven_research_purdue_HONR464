## Purpose

Rehearsal is the difference between nerves and panic. Today you stand your poster
through a simulated conference round — pitches, hard questions, and ULN moments,
all under time — and you visit others as a critical-but-kind stranger. By the end
you sign a readiness audit: every piece confirmed, or a named plan to fix it
tonight.

## Materials

- Your poster (printed, or full-screen if a print failed).
- Both pitches (1-minute and 3-minute) and your question bank, cold.
- The visitor role card (below) — one per person.
- The readiness audit sheet (below) — one per person, signed at the end.

## Station-round format

Two 20-minute rounds. In each round, **2–3 posters are live** — those presenters
stand and present; everyone else + Professor Moreira rotate as conference visitors.
Across the two rounds every poster goes live and every person visits. Live
minutes exercise all four skills: the 1-minute hook, the 3-minute walk, hard
questions from the bank and beyond, and the ULN moment.

## The visitor role card

```
CONFERENCE VISITOR — role card
Play a critical-but-kind stranger. Across your visits today:
  ☐ Ask at least one METHOD question
  ☐ Ask at least one ALTERNATIVE-EXPLANATION question
  ☐ Ask at least one SO-WHAT question
  ☐ Ask at least one GENERALIZATION question
  ☐ Ask at least one AI-USE question
After each visit, tell the presenter ONE thing that landed and ONE thing to tighten.
```

Spread the five types across your visits — do not ask the same easy question at
every poster. The presenter needs to feel each type before tomorrow.

## The readiness audit sheet (sign in class)

```
READINESS AUDIT — M12 Expo readiness   Name: __________________   Date: Nov 16

  ☐ Rehearsed the full walk TWICE, inside 3 minutes
  ☐ Question bank DRILLED (survived a hot seat / today's visitors)
  ☐ LOGISTICS confirmed: location, call time, dress, travel
  ☐ MATERIALS ready: poster (printed or full-screen), handout if any, backup file

The ONE thing I will do differently tomorrow because of today:
_____________________________________________________________

Signed: __________________
```

You sign only what is true. An unchecked box is not a failure — it is a task with
a deadline of tonight. "I'll wing it tomorrow" is the claim this audit forbids.

## Procedure (50 minutes)

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–4 | Rehearsal norms: run it like it's real; notes after, not during. |
| 4–24 | **Station round 1** (20 min): 2–3 posters live; the rest visit with the role card. |
| 24–44 | **Station round 2** (20 min): the remaining posters live; rotate. |
| 44–48 | **Readiness audit:** sign the sheet (rehearsed / drilled / logistics / materials + the one thing). |
| 48–50 | Final word + tomorrow's call time. |

## Roles

- **Presenter (when live):** run it exactly as you will tomorrow; take notes after your round, not during.
- **Visitor:** work the five-type card, give one-landed / one-to-tighten after each stop.

### Session flow (for the record)

Professor Moreira sets up the room as stations, distributes visitor cards, times the
two 20-minute rounds, confirms Expo logistics (time, place, setup), and
communicates the call time. Each poster goes live once; each person visits across
both rounds and signs one audit.

## Common failure modes

1. **Rehearsing soft.** A half-effort run trains nothing. Present as if the stranger's opinion counts — tomorrow it will.
2. **Notes during the run.** Interrupting to fix a line breaks the simulation. Hold notes for after the round.
3. **Same question every visit.** One easy question at five posters helps no one. Work the whole five-type card.
4. **Signing a false box.** An audit that lies is worse than an honest gap. Sign only what's true; the unchecked box is tonight's task.
5. **Print assumed, not confirmed.** If the print isn't in hand, present full-screen today and trigger the emergency print run — the backup plan resolves it before Tuesday.
