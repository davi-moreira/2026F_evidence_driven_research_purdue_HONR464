semester (it is re-submitted, updated, inside M07, M09, and the final dossier).
Every section is required. This document is your standing answer to the question
"what KIND of inquiry is this, and how far does it REACH?" — the course's
signature skill, in writing.*

## 2. My inquiry, on the compass

**KIND — I classify my question as (choose one):**

- [ ] **Descriptive** — it asks *what the world is or was*; answering it needs
  no counterfactual contrast.
- [ ] **Causal** — it asks *what an intervention would change*; answering it
  requires comparing the world to a counterfactual.

**REACH — my answer must hold for (choose one):**

- [ ] **The data at hand** — the units I actually observe (description).
- [ ] **A population beyond the data** — the units my sampling frame reaches
  (generalization, with quantified uncertainty).
- [ ] **Cases not yet seen** — future or out-of-sample units (prediction,
  against a stated baseline and metric).

**Secondary compass position (only if the question genuinely requires one):**

## 3. Why this classification fits (the justification)

> *Three to six sentences. Point at the words in your question that fix its kind
> and its reach. Name at least one neighboring position and say why it is NOT the
> right fit — the strongest declarations show the road not taken.*

## 4. My crossing licenses

## 5. What my answer WILL be able to establish

> *Write the strongest claim your position permits, as a sentence you hope to
> say in December. Description: "In these data…". Generalization: "…with stated
> uncertainty, for the population my frame reaches." Prediction: "…on held-out
> cases, against the baseline…". Causal: "…under the identification argument
> that…".*

> *Equally concrete. The claim a careless reader might take away that your
> design does not license — usually a compass crossing you hold no license for.
> Write it out and forbid it in your own words.*
