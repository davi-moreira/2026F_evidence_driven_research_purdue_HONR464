# ASSESSMENT_ARCHITECTURE — what is graded, why, and how it adds up

Reconciled weights for `syllabus.qmd` (replaces the seeded 8-row provisional
table). Philosophy from the brief: **no conventional midterm or final exam** —
the course assesses the research chain itself. Grading rewards correctness,
transparency, reproducibility, approach-alignment, and responsible
interpretation — **never coding elegance** (the course assumes no computing
background; all machinery is provided).

## The six components (100%)

| Component | Weight | What it contains |
|---|---:|---|
| **1. Daily notebook preparation & engagement** | **15%** | Notebook completion (executed cells + written responses), Claim Tickets (44), studio participation, red-team/cross-examination citizenship |
| **2. Milestone development, presentations & revisions** | **30%** | M00–M09, M13–M15, M17–M18 artifacts + their in-class presentations + the revision habit |
| **3. URC poster & presentation** | **20%** | M10 storyboard (2%), M11 draft + red-team (3%), M12 final poster (8%), M16 Expo presentation (7%) |
| **4. Post-conference final research dossier** | **20%** | M19 sensitivity + claim ledger (5%), M20 reproducibility audit (5%), M21 research brief (4%), M23 final dossier (6%) |
| **5. Evidence Defense & course synthesis** | **5%** | M22 defense (4%) + M44 synthesis exercise & exit self-assessment (1%) |
| **6. Claim analyses & concept checks** | **10%** | nb08 async claim diagnosis (4%), three short concept checks across the term (4.5%), optional Calling Bullshit analyses (1.5% — replaceable by an extra concept check if CB is skipped, per the locked "optional CB" decision) |

Component 2 internal weighting: the 14 milestones in the bucket are equally
weighted except M07 (double — it contains both the abstract gate and the full
declaration) and M15 (half — a signed audit). Presentations count inside each
milestone's rubric (a "presented + incorporated feedback" rubric row), so
presenting is graded where it happens, not as a separate ledger.

## Why these weights

- **Development outweighs any single artifact** (30% vs 8% for the poster
  itself): the course's claim is that the *chain* — declare, diagnose, redesign,
  verify — is the learning, and the weights say so out loud.
- **Post-conference work is substantive by construction** (25% across components
  4–5 lands after Nov 17), enforcing the brief's quality gate that the semester
  does not end at the Expo.
- **Engagement is 15% because the pedagogy is ≥70% active**: a student who
  skips the studios has skipped the course.
- **Nothing is auto-graded MC** by default: the MC option-length parity rule and
  `audit_answer_length.py` stay dormant unless concept checks move to
  Brightspace auto-grading later (allowed, not planned).

## Rubric DNA (shared across all milestone rubrics)

Every milestone rubric in `_research_project/2026Fall/` draws its criteria rows
from this fixed menu, so the same virtues are rewarded all semester:

1. **Approach alignment** — the work matches the declared approach and stays
   inside its claim boundary (CLAUDE.md Method-Selection rule).
2. **Evidence integrity** — every empirical claim traces to a real, retrievable
   source or a reproducible computation (CLAUDE.md Evidence-Integrity rule).
3. **Verification** — the deliverable records how outputs were cross-checked;
   AI use is disclosed Ask → Verify → Document (CLAUDE.md Responsible-AI rule).
4. **Uncertainty & limitations** — stated, calibrated, neither hidden nor
   spiraling (CLAUDE.md Uncertainty rule).
5. **Craft & communication** — organized, on-format, on-time, audience-aware.

## Grade bands & policies

Letter bands follow the standard Purdue scale (A ≥ 93, A− ≥ 90, B+ ≥ 87, B ≥ 83,
B− ≥ 80, C+ ≥ 77, C ≥ 73, C− ≥ 70, D ≥ 60, F < 60; no curve planned for n=5).
Late policy: −10%/day to 3 days, then 0 (terminal artifacts M12/M16/M22/M23:
deadline governs; documented emergencies handled individually per Purdue
policy). The Daniels School GPA-3.3 cap does **not** apply (Honors College
course — removed from the seeded syllabus; verify once with the Honors College
before publishing, tracked on the course board).

## Integrity instruments

- **AI-use ledger** — every deliverable carries the disclosure block (tool,
  task, verification); undisclosed AI use is an academic-integrity violation
  (syllabus AI policy).
- **Reproduction sign-off** (M20) — a classmate attests your headline number
  reproduces from your package alone.
- **Claim ledger** (M19→M23) — every claim's row: claim, evidence,
  verification, boundary, sensitivity survival.
