## Purpose

This is where the whole semester becomes one artifact. The dossier is not a
report *about* your project — it **is** your project: the complete, ordered,
verified record of how a curiosity became a defended, reproducible, honestly
bounded claim. Every earlier milestone was a component being built to
tolerance; the dossier assembles them and holds them to the course's three
standards at once: **everything claimed is verified, everything verified is
documented, and uncertainty and limitations are stated throughout.** The
**claim ledger is its spine** — the through-line that connects your question to
your evidence to your boundary, and the object your Evidence Defense was
performed against. Because M23 is terminal, this is also the last chance to close
what the defense exposed. Assemble deliberately, using the final-dossier
checklist below, and submit something you would hand to a stranger and trust
them to reach your conclusions.

## Components

### 1. The complete arc

The dossier carries the whole chain in order — each part already built at its
milestone, now finalized and reconciled with everything the defense taught you:

- **Question + approach declaration (final form)** — the research question and
  the quantitative approach(es) it demands, with the method-selection
  justification (why this family, what it can and cannot establish, its
  limitations).
- **Verified source map** — the literature/claim map with every source real and
  retrievable (M02).
- **Full design declaration** — Model, Inquiry, Data, Answer (M03–M07).
- **Diagnosis + redesign history** — the design diagnosis (M08) and the
  feedback-to-redesign plan (M18), including what you changed and what you
  wouldn't.
- **Pilot + sensitivity analyses** — the pilot (M09) and the approach-branched
  sensitivity checks (M19), with what moved.
- **The poster** — as presented at the Expo (M12/M16).
- **The conference record + redesign plan** — the coded reflection (M17) and the
  triaged plan (M18).
- **The reproducibility package with its signed attestation** — the M20 package
  and the partner sign-off (or honest residuals log).
- **The research brief** — the one-page brief (M21).
- **The complete claim ledger** — see Component 2.

### 2. The complete claim ledger (the spine)

Every claim the dossier makes appears in the ledger, each row with the five
fields established at M19: **claim · evidence · verification · boundary ·
survived sensitivity?** By M23 the ledger is complete and reconciled — no claim
in the prose that lacks a row, no row contradicted by the prose. Where the
Evidence Defense forced a concession, the ledger reflects it. This is the object
a reader (or a future you) uses to check that the dossier keeps its promises.

**Strong ledger row (concrete example):** *Claim — "In my pilot of 320 first-year
respondents, first-generation respondents reported skipping office hours about
18 percentage points more often than continuing-generation respondents."*
Evidence — nb17 cell 14. Verification — recomputed by hand; reproduced by a
partner on Dec 2 (M20). Boundary — descriptive, these 320 respondents only.
Survived sensitivity? — Yes; held at 15 points under an alternative binning.

**Weak ledger row (non-example):** *Claim — "First-gen undergraduates avoid office
hours." Evidence — "the survey." Verification — (blank). Boundary — (blank).
Survived sensitivity? — "yes."* A generalization the pilot can't support, with
no source, no verification, and no boundary. In a terminal dossier this is not a
row that can be revised later — it must be right now.

### 3. The final-dossier checklist (your assembly guide)

Run this checklist (you first run it in class at M43) as you assemble. Every box
should be true before you submit:

- [ ] **Notebook runs clean** — restart-and-run-all, no manual steps (M20).
- [ ] **Every claim in the prose has a ledger row**, and every ledger row appears
  in the prose.
- [ ] **Every empirical claim traces to a real, retrievable source or a
  reproducible computation** — no unverifiable or hallucinated citations.
- [ ] **Every headline result records how it was verified** (recomputed,
  cross-checked, or partner-reproduced).
- [ ] **The approach is justified** — why this family, what it can/cannot
  establish, its limitations.
- [ ] **Uncertainty and limitations appear throughout**, not just in a closing
  paragraph.
- [ ] **The signed reproduction attestation (or honest residuals log) is
  included** (M20).
- [ ] **Defense exposures are closed** — what cross-examination revealed is
  reflected in the ledger and prose.
- [ ] **AI-use disclosure block is complete and finalized** (project-scope
  ledger).
- [ ] **Filenames and format** — `lastname_m23_dossier.pdf` + `.ipynb`.

### 4. AI-use disclosure block (required on every deliverable)

A short table (finalized to project scope): which AI tool(s) you used, for what
task (locate / draft / critique / none), and how you verified the output. "No AI
used" is a fine entry; an undisclosed AI contribution is an academic-integrity
violation.

## Penalties

- Late: the deadline governs (terminal artifact). Per Purdue policy, the late
  scale applies as published; documented emergencies are handled individually —
  talk to me first.
- Any cited source that turns out not to exist or not to say what you claim: the
  Evidence-integrity criterion scores Beginning (0–7) regardless of the rest —
  the course's evidence-integrity rule, applied to the terminal artifact.
- A claim in the prose with no ledger row (or a ledger row the prose
  contradicts) drops the Claim-ledger criterion to Developing or below.
- Missing AI-disclosure block or a non-running notebook: Craft scores 0 and the
  submission is incomplete.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **The stapled dossier.** A pile of the earlier milestones in a single PDF is
   not a dossier. It has to be *reconciled* — one arc, one ledger, no
   contradictions between parts.
2. **The un-updated ledger.** If the Evidence Defense made you concede a limit
   and the ledger still claims the old boundary, the dossier is internally at
   war with itself. Close the loop.
3. **Uncertainty as an afterthought.** A single "limitations" paragraph at the
   end is not "throughout." The uncertainty belongs next to each claim it
   qualifies.
4. **The notebook that no longer runs.** Terminal means you don't get to fix it
   Monday. Restart-and-run-all before you submit, every time.
5. **A resurrected caveat-free claim.** The brief disciplined it, the defense
   probed it — don't let it sneak back into the dossier clean. The ledger is the
   final check.
6. **Waiting for the ceremony.** M44 confirms submission; it does not extend the
   deadline. The 11:59 PM clock is the one that grades.
