## Purpose

A poster is read by people who came to look; an abstract is read by people
deciding whether to look. A **research brief** is read by a **decision-maker who
will never see your appendix** and has about ninety seconds. This milestone
compresses fourteen weeks onto **one page** — question, finding, uncertainty,
implication, one figure — for exactly that reader. The hard discipline is that
compression must not become un-verification: **every sentence on the page traces
to a row in your claim ledger.** A brief is allowed to say less than the dossier;
it is never allowed to say *more*. The failure this milestone is built to
prevent is the quiet one — a caveated, disciplined finding from the ledger
sliding back into a clean, confident, caveat-free sentence because the clean
version reads better. If a sentence upgrades the claim, the ledger must be
upgraded first, with evidence. Otherwise the caveat stays on the page.

## Components

### 1. The one page (for a decision-maker who won't see the appendix)

A single page, built for a reader with power and no time. It contains, clearly:

- **The question** — in plain language a non-specialist acts on.
- **The finding** — what you found, at its real boundary.
- **The uncertainty — on the page, not in the appendix.** The one thing the
  reader most needs to know about how firm this is.
- **The implication** — the "so what" for the reader's decision.
- **One figure** — honest (scale, base, missing cases), and legible at a glance.

### 2. Ledger traceability

Every sentence traces to a **claim-ledger row** (from M19). Nothing on the page
exceeds what the ledger licenses. When you compress three caveated sentences
into one, the caveat rides along.

**Strong finding sentence (concrete example):** *"In my pilot of 320 first-year
respondents, first-generation respondents reported skipping office hours about
18 percentage points more often than their peers — a gap large enough to matter
if it holds in the full population, which this pilot cannot yet confirm."* It
states the number, the sample boundary, and the uncertainty in one breath, and
every clause maps to a ledger row.

**Weak finding sentence (non-example):** *"First-generation undergraduates avoid
office hours."* Cleaner, shorter — and a lie by compression: it drops the units,
the sample boundary, and the uncertainty the ledger insists on. This is the
"caveat-free restoration" the milestone exists to catch.

### 3. The table read: margin notes received + your revision

At M41 all five briefs are read by everyone, each reader leaving two margin
notes: the **sharpest sentence** and the **weakest claim**. Include the notes
you received and your **revision** in response. If a reader flagged your weakest
claim, the revision should show the ledger disciplining it — not cosmetic
wordsmithing.

### 4. AI-use disclosure block (required on every deliverable)

A short table: which AI tool(s) you used, for what task (locate / draft /
critique / none), and how you verified the output. "No AI used" is a fine
entry; an undisclosed AI contribution is an academic-integrity violation.

## Grading Rubric (100 points)

| Criterion | Exemplary | Proficient | Developing | Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Reader fit & compression** (20) | One page a busy decision-maker could act on in 90 seconds; nothing extraneous (18–20) | Mostly reader-fit; a little too much or too dense (14–17) | Reads like an abstract or a shrunk poster, not a brief (8–13) | Over a page, or written for a specialist (0–7) |
| **Ledger traceability & evidence integrity** (25) | Every sentence traces to a ledger row; nothing exceeds what the evidence licenses (22–25) | Traceable; one sentence slightly ahead of its row (18–21) | Some sentences untraceable to any ledger row (11–17) | Claims on the page the ledger does not support (0–10) |

## Penalties

- Late: −10 points per day, up to 3 days; not accepted after (documented
  emergencies: talk to me first, per the syllabus).
- A sentence that upgrades a claim beyond its ledger row (without a
  correspondingly upgraded, evidenced ledger) drops the Uncertainty criterion to
  Beginning — this is the course's evidence-integrity rule applied to
  compression.
- Missing AI-disclosure block: Craft scores 0 and the submission is returned
  for completion before grading.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **The caveat that fell off in editing.** The single most common failure: a
   disciplined ledger claim reappears clean and confident because the clean
   version scans better. Put the caveat back.
2. **The shrunk poster.** A brief is not a poster at 40%. It is written for a
   different reader with a different need — a decision, not a browse.
3. **Uncertainty in the appendix.** Your reader never opens the appendix. If the
   uncertainty isn't on the one page, for them it doesn't exist.
4. **The decorative figure.** One figure, and it must earn its space by carrying
   the finding honestly — not by looking impressive.
5. **Ignoring the weakest-claim note.** The table-read reader who flagged your
   weakest claim did you a favor. Revising the *sharpest* sentence instead of the
   weakest claim misses the point.
