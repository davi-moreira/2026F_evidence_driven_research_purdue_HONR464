## Purpose

Tomorrow is the real thing. Today is the last chance to find out — in a safe
room, with critical-but-kind classmates as stand-in "conference visitors" —
whether every piece actually works when you have to run it live: the poster
stood, the 1-minute and 3-minute pitches delivered, the ULN move spoken, the
hard questions drilled, and the logistics nailed down. Rehearsal is the
difference between nerves and panic. "I'll wing it tomorrow" is the one thing
this milestone exists to make impossible.

The audit is a completion-and-quality check: you either did the rehearsal and
confirmed the logistics, or you did not. Sign only what is true.

## Components

Each line of the readiness audit is confirmed **in class** and signed:

### 1. Rehearsed twice, in stations

You stood your poster and delivered your pitches through **two** full simulation
rounds, with visitors rotating past — the 1-minute hook, the 3-minute walk, the
transitions between pitch and Q&A all exercised under something like real
conditions.

### 2. Question bank drilled

Your five-question bank (M14) was drilled against live questions — including
surprises you did not write — and you handled the hard ones with boundary
discipline, including at least one honest don't-know delivered out loud.

### 3. Logistics confirmed

The four logistics facts are written down and verified, not assumed: **time**
(your call time and session window), **place** (room/location and your setup
spot), **dress** (what you are wearing), and **materials** (poster, handouts,
anything you carry).

### 4. Print backup plan

A concrete fallback if the print fails: where and by when you can reprint, or the
full-screen presentation you will stand instead. "It'll be fine" is not a plan;
"emergency print at the library by 8 a.m., PDF loaded on my laptop as backup" is.

- ✓ **A real readiness line:** *"Rehearsed twice ✓; drilled the generalization
  question and gave the honest don't-know ✓; call time 12:45, Stewart 214,
  business casual, poster tube + 5 handouts ✓; backup = laptop full-screen +
  library reprint slot 8 a.m."*
- ⚠️ **A hollow readiness line:** *"Basically ready, I'll sort the details out
  tomorrow morning."* — nothing confirmed, nothing signed truthfully; this is
  the state the audit exists to catch.

### AI-use note

If any rehearsal prep used an AI tool (for example, generating surprise practice
questions), note it in one line on the audit sheet's disclosure field — same
Ask → Verify → Document habit, kept alive even on an in-class milestone.

## Penalties

- **Absent from M35:** the rehearsal cannot be made up in the room it requires;
  documented emergencies are handled individually per Purdue policy (talk to me
  first), and an unexcused absence forfeits the rehearsal credit.
- **Signing an untrue line:** attesting to a rehearsal round or a logistics fact
  that did not happen is an integrity issue, not a shortcut — the audit is only
  worth anything if the signature means something.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **"I'll wing it tomorrow."** The exact sentence this milestone forbids.
   Winging a live audience is how prepared work gets undersold in 60 seconds.
2. **Logistics left to the morning.** Not knowing your call time or setup spot
   turns Tuesday's first hour into avoidable panic. Write the four facts today.
3. **No print backup.** Printers fail the night before conferences as a law of
   nature. Have the reprint slot and the full-screen fallback ready.
4. **Rehearsing only the parts you like.** Running the hook ten times and never
   the hard-question drill. Rehearse the Q&A most, because that is what you can
   least control tomorrow.
5. **Signing to look ready.** The audit protects you only if you sign what is
   true. A half-honest sheet is worse than an honest "not yet" you still have
   hours to fix.
