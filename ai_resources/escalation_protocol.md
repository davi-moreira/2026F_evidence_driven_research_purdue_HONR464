# Escalation Protocol

*When AI work must stop and come back to you. Delegation has a hard boundary:
past it, the tool informs but you decide. This page names the five triggers that
force an escalation and gives the ledger format for recording it. AI is your arm
and your research assistant, not your brain.*

---

## What "escalation" means here

Escalation is the moment you take the pen back. You may have delegated a task,
run several roles, and gathered good material. Then the work reaches a point where
continuing to let AI drive would hand away a decision that is yours. At that point
you **stop delegating and decide**, in your own words, with your own verification,
on the record.

Escalation is not a failure of the AI. It is the system working. The whole design
of the course puts a human at exactly these points on purpose.

## The five triggers

Escalate the moment any one of these is true. You do not wait for all five; one is
enough.

**1. The output touches a never-delegate decision.** If an AI output would
*settle* rather than *inform* any item on the
[human responsibility checklist](human_responsibility_checklist.md) — the research
problem, the design and population, the measurement, an ethical judgment, the
claim boundary, the stated uncertainty, or the public defense — you stop and
decide it yourself.

**2. Verification fails, or cannot be performed.** If a named method from the
[verification guide](verification_guide.md) contradicts the output, or if there is
no way to verify it at all, the output does not get used. An unverifiable result
is not a small problem to note; it is a stop.

**3. Two roles disagree on a consequential point.** When two AI roles clash on
something that would change a claim, a design, or a number, you do not let the
tools resolve it and you do not take a vote. You diagnose the conflict (real
disagreement, correlated error, or false consensus) and decide, per the
[multi-agent worksheet](multi_agent_worksheet.md).

**4. The output would change a declared design.** If following the AI's suggestion
would alter your inquiry declaration, your compass position, your identification
argument, or your analysis protocol, that is a design decision, not an
implementation detail. It comes back to you and, if it stands, into a documented
revision of the declaration.

**5. Ethics.** Anything touching the fair treatment of subjects, honest reporting,
consent, privacy, or responsible framing escalates immediately and always. There
is no version of an ethical call that AI makes for you.

## What to do when you escalate

1. **Stop using the output** as if it were decided. It is now an input, not an
   answer.
2. **Make the decision yourself**, in your own words, and be ready to defend it to
   a skeptical human without citing the tool.
3. **Verify** your decision with a named, preferably non-AI method.
4. **Record the escalation** in your [AI Research Ledger](ai_research_ledger_template.md)
   using the format below.
5. If the decision changes a declared design, **update the declaration** and note
   the revision.

## The escalation entry format

An escalation is a ledger row like any other, but its decision column carries more
weight and it names the trigger. Use this shape:

```
| Task delegated | Tool used | Prompt | Output summary | Decision | Verification method | Remaining concern | Responsible student |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [the task that hit the boundary] | [tool/role] | [prompt] | [what it returned] | ESCALATED (trigger #_): I took this decision myself — [your decision, in your words, and why the AI output did not settle it] | [the non-AI method you used to stand behind your decision] | [what you still watch] | You |
```

The tell of a good escalation row is that the **Decision** column reads like a
person taking responsibility, not a tool being quoted. Compare:

- ✓ *"ESCALATED (trigger #1): the role proposed calling my result causal. I kept
  the claim descriptive because my design has no identification argument; verified
  by re-reading my inquiry declaration."*
- ⚠️ *"The AI said to make it causal, so I did."* — this is the failure the
  protocol exists to prevent: a never-delegate decision handed to the tool.

## Why this is graded

Escalation is not extra credit for caution; it is the course's central habit made
visible. A semester-long ledger with **no** escalations is a warning sign, not a
badge. It usually means either AI was never used on anything that mattered, or a
boundary was crossed silently. Your M15 AI-management portfolio is read partly for
whether you recognized these boundaries and took the pen back when you should
have.
